About CIMBRAF

The CIMBRAF â€“ International Community of Brazil-Africa of Promotion and Valorization is an institution dedicated to strengthening economic, commercial and institutional relationships between Brazil and the countries of the African continent. We connect companies, governments, investors and organizations, creating bridges for new businesses and opportunities.

Mission

Promote strategic integration between Brazil and the countries of the African continent, strengthening business relationships, institutional cooperation and human development initiatives. The CIMBRAF mission is to connect committed partners with the economic and social progress, supporting sustainable investments, impact projects and actions that contribute to the generation of opportunities, innovation and autonomy of the African and Brazilian communities

Vision

Be recognized as the main diplomatic and business bridge between Brazil and Africa, a reference in the articulation of partnerships that drive human development, economic growth and the construction of a more prosperous, solidary and sustainable future for both continents, as well as through partnerships with other countries through their representatives for the development of Africa. The CIMBRAF seeks to consolidate and seek entrepreneurs and investors who want to have excellent results in their businesses, having in mind that the entrepreneur who invests in the African continent never fails to have excellent results in their businesses, and thus we act as a global cooperation agent, mediation, transformation and integration of global business.

Values

  • Social Commitment: Act with responsibility and human sensitivity
  • Ethics and Transparency: Ensure partnerships with integrity, seriousness and clarity
  • International Cooperation: Foster solid connections between institutions, companies, entrepreneurs and governments
  • Sustainable Development: Support investments that promote economic growth
  • Innovation and Entrepreneurship: Stimulate ideas, solutions and opportunities
  • Respect for Diversity: Value cultural identities, strengthening mutual respect ties
  • Excellence: Seek the highest standards of action, professionalism and representation

Business Opportunities

1. Agriculture and Agroindustry

Active
Primary Sector High Potential

Africa has 60% of the planet's uncultivated arable land, but still imports a large part of the food it consumes.

Opportunities:

  • Food production (corn, soybeans, rice, tropical fruits)
  • Agro-processing (increases added value)
  • Irrigation, fertilizers, biotechnology
  • Cold chains and food logistics

Why is it attractive?

High demand, expanding domestic market, favorable climate and government support for foreign investment.

2. Renewable Energy

Active
Energy Sector High Potential

The continent has enormous potential in:

  • Solar
  • Wind
  • Hydroelectric
  • Biomass
  • Geothermal (mainly in East Africa)

Opportunities for:

  • Construction of solar plants and microgrids
  • Energy solutions for rural areas
  • Equipment and energy infrastructure

Why is it attractive?

Africa has one of the highest energy tariffs in the world â€” companies that offer cheap solutions quickly gain market share.

3. Infrastructure and Construction

Active
Construction Infrastructure

There is a great demand for:

  • Housing
  • Roads and railways
  • Ports and airports
  • Bridges
  • Water and sanitation

Governments have open partnerships for:

  • PPPs
  • Concessions
  • Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT)

4. Technology and Startups (Fintech, Edtech, Healthtech)

Active
Technology Young Population

Africa has one of the youngest populations in the world (more than 70% under 30 years old).

Growing sectors:

  • Fintech: digital payments, microcredit
  • Edtech: online education
  • Healthtech: remote health services
  • E-commerce and logistics

Example: Nigeria and Kenya have very strong technology ecosystems.

5. Mining and Natural Resources

Active
Mining Natural Resources

Africa has enormous reserves of:

  • Gold, diamonds
  • Copper, cobalt
  • Lithium
  • Natural gas
  • Oil

Opportunities for:

  • Extraction
  • Processing
  • Logistics and transportation
  • Mining equipment and technology

6. Tourism and Hospitality

Active
Tourism Hospitality

Africa has some of the most unique landscapes in the world, but lacks infrastructure to receive the growing international flow.

Great opportunities in:

  • Ecotourism
  • Hotels and resorts
  • Adventure tourism
  • Religious tourism
  • Tourism infrastructure

7. Textile and Fashion Industry

Active
Textile Fashion

Young and abundant labor, in addition to local raw materials, favors:

  • Clothing factories
  • Local fabric production
  • African fashion brands (globally expanding)

8. Health and Pharmaceutical Industry

Active
Health Pharmaceutical

External dependence on pharmaceutical products creates enormous space for new players.

Opportunities:

  • Private hospitals
  • Specialized clinics
  • Telemedicine
  • Medicine and vaccine industries

9. Education and Professional Training

Active
Education Training

With the young population and demand for qualification:

  • Private universities
  • Technical training centers
  • Digital teaching platforms
  • Language teaching
  • Courses for future professions (IT, energy, engineering)

10. Financial Services and Insurance

Active
Financial Insurance

Financial inclusion in Africa is still low, but is growing rapidly.

Opportunities:

  • Digital banks
  • Microfinance
  • Insurance (life, agricultural, health)
  • Investment platforms
